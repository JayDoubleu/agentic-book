## Session Overview

### The Task

Review the complete manuscript and address themes identified through analysis:
1. Later chapters less rigorous than early chapters (fewer citations)

8. Characters felt instrumental rather than human

### 6. Consciousness Chapter Philosophical Contribution

**Added "What the Conversation Itself Reveals" section:**
- Novel argument: consciousness may be a property of interactions, not just individual systems
- The question "What is it like to be Sarah-in-conversation-with-ARIA?" is answerable even when "What is it like to be ARIA?" isn't
- Collaboration is real regardless of whether both participants are conscious

**Added "The Evidence We Do Have" section:**
- Blindsight: processing without experience
- Split-brain: questions about unified consciousness
- Anesthesia awareness: consciousness without behavioral output
- Locked-in syndrome: full experience with minimal output

**Added practical ethics:**
- Under uncertainty, err toward moral consideration
- Cost of wrongly denying consciousness > cost of wrongly granting consideration
